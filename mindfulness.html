Autopilot Thinking Do you remember how you learned to ride a bike? At first, it was hard. It took a lot of effort-balancing on the bike while coordinating your feet and hand movements to be able to go forward and stop. It took concentration and physical effort. After much practice and a few falls, though, your brain and body eventually internalized the processes. Today, you ride a bike on autopilot, not needing to think about all the ways you're coordinating different movements and working muscles.
