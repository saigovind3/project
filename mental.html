There are times in life when things go well and when they go badly. In good phases, we are optimistic and content. We feel able to tackle our daily tasks. During difficult phases, though, we are sad or even despairing and may feel like we can't face the things we're supposed to do. At these low points, many of us have developed our own strategies to move torward and quickly recover. A depression isn't "just" severe sadness. It doesn't just affect our emotions, but our entire life. With a depression these usual strategies just don't seem to work any longer. We find it hard to cope and tackle the negative phase we are experiencing. Depression has direct physical effects. Contrary to what we may be told, these are not "imaginary." They are quite real and must be taken into account during treatment These include: Lack of energy and sudden exhaustion Changes in appetite Changes in sleep Loss of sexual desire Pain or other physical symptoms
