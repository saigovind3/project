Depression can change regular ways of thinking. For many, this means increased thoughts about death whether in a general sense such as contemplating the finite nature of life or thinking more specifically such as about people who have died. Thoughts of one's own death from suicide or even an accident or illness can also be quite common. During a depressive episode, feelings of hopelessness, despair, sadness, or inner emptiness can be overwhelming. Thinking processes can also slow or be less productive, making it difficult to find solutions to problems. With all this, it is not surprising that some people think of death as a way out when suffering with depression. The good news here is that there are strategies that have proven successful for mitigating suicidal thoughts. This course will introduce you to some that can relieve you in the short term. There are many more that can help in the long run that you can learn in therapy with the support of a mental health practitioner. Having an emergency plan in place when suicidal thoughts come up can be an important first step, which is the focus of the next chapter. The chapters that follow will take you through some of the short-term strategies to help deter suicidal thoughts.
